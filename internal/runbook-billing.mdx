---
title: "Billing and subscription runbook"
description: "Internal runbook for troubleshooting billing, subscription, and trial issues."
---

## Scope

Use this runbook when:

- Organizations report billing problems (for example, unexpected plan changes, seat counts, or failed payments).
- Trials behave unexpectedly (ending too early, not starting, or not converting).
- Access is restricted due to payment status and needs investigation.

## 1. Triage

- Identify:
  - The organization and primary contact.
  - The current plan, seat counts, and modules (if known).
  - Whether this is a one-off issue or part of a broader incident.
- Collect:
  - Screenshots of what the admin sees in the billing area.
  - Any email notifications about payments or failed charges.

## 2. Trial-related issues

- Confirm:
  - Trial start and expected end dates.
  - Whether the org has previously converted or extended trials.
- Check:
  - Whether trial state is accurately reflected in the app (for example, banners, warnings).

## 3. Subscription and payment issues

- Verify:
  - Current subscription status (active, past due, canceled).
  - Recent payment attempts and outcomes (via your payment provider).
- For access restrictions:
  - Confirm whether they align with current status (for example, payment-required screens for past-due accounts).

## 4. Seat counts and modules

- Review:
  - Seat counts by role (technician, PM, etc.).
  - Enabled modules and any recent changes.
- Check:
  - That UI reflects what is configured in billing.
  - Whether recent upgrades or downgrades have fully completed.

## 5. Communication and remediation

- Communicate clearly with the customer:
  - What you’ve found.
  - Whether this is a configuration issue, a payment provider problem, or a product bug.
- After fixing the issue:
  - Update or correct subscription data as needed.
  - Capture root cause and follow-up items (for example, improved validation or monitoring).
