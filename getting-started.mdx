## Who this is for

This page is for people who are **new to Fieldgrid** and want a fast, product‑level overview before diving into the admin or technician guides.

If you already know your role:

- Start with **Admin getting started** if you run operations.
- Start with **Technician getting started** if you do work in the field.

## Prerequisites

- A Fieldgrid account with access to at least one organization.
- Permissions to create customers, projects, and jobs (typically an admin or manager role).

## Step 1 – Log in and select your organization

1. Go to the Fieldgrid web app.
2. Sign in with your work email.
3. If you have access to multiple organizations, choose the one you want to work in from the organization selector.

If you’re the first person from your company, follow **Admin onboarding: create a new organization** first.

## Step 2 – Create a customer, project, and job

Fieldgrid models work as **Customer → Project → Job → Work Orders**.

1. From the admin dashboard, create a **customer** (for example, a real or test client).
2. Under that customer, create a **project** (for example, “HVAC service and maintenance”).
3. Inside the project, create a **job** (for example, “Quarterly service – Main building”).
4. Add one or more **work orders** if you need to break the job into visits or technician‑level tasks.

This gives you a realistic test case that matches how Fieldgrid is used in production.

## Step 3 – Invite your team

1. Open your **Team** or **Users** area from the admin navigation.
2. Invite collaborators by email.
3. Assign them roles based on what they need to do:
   - **Technicians** focused on completing work orders and logging evidence.
   - **Admins / coordinators** focused on planning work, managing schedules, and reviewing data.
   - **Owners / leaders** who need read‑heavy access to dashboards and reports.

Teammates will follow the **Technician onboarding** or role‑specific getting‑started guides once they accept their invites.

## Step 4 – Run a simple end‑to‑end workflow

To see Fieldgrid in action from both the office and field perspectives:

1. As an admin:
   - Follow **Admin job lifecycle** to move your test job through creation, assignment, and completion.
2. As a technician (or test technician account):
   - Follow **Technician daily workflow** to:
     - See the assigned job.
     - Complete work orders.
     - Add notes and photos.
3. Back as an admin:
   - Review time, parts, and any expenses (if configured).
   - Practice creating a **draft invoice** using **Admin invoicing and billing**.

This gives you a full loop from work planning to billing on a safe, test job.

- Follow **Admin getting started** or **Technician getting started** for a deeper first‑week path.
- Explore the **API reference** if you plan to integrate Fieldgrid with existing tools or internal systems.
