## Organizations, users, and roles

Fieldgrid is designed for multi‑tenant use: everything happens inside an **organization**.

- **Organizations**
  - Represent each customer account that uses Fieldgrid.
  - Own projects, jobs, users, and configuration.
- **Users**
  - People who log into Fieldgrid.
  - Have roles such as **owner**, **admin**, **technician**, **project manager**, or **supervisor**.
  - Can belong to one or more organizations.
- **Roles and permissions**
  - Control what someone can see and do.
  - For example, an admin may manage jobs, invoices, and team members, while a technician focuses on their assigned work in the field.

## Customers, projects, jobs, and work orders

Fieldgrid models your work in a simple hierarchy:

> **Customer → Project → Job → Work Order**, with **Jobsites** attached to jobs.

- **Customers**
  - The clients your organization serves.
  - Hold key details such as contact information and basic service context.
- **Projects**
  - Group related work for a customer.
  - Have dates, budgets, and a project owner.
- **Jobs**
  - Concrete pieces of work within a project (for example, “Install rooftop unit,” “Commission system,” or “Annual service visit”).
  - Track status, priority, and assignments.
- **Work Orders**
  - Task‑level units of work technicians complete in the field.
  - Make it clear who is doing what, where, and by when.
- **Jobsites**
  - The physical locations where work takes place.
  - Can be linked to one or more jobs and work orders.

Together, these concepts let Fieldgrid show you exactly what work is happening for which customer, where, and under which project.

## Time tracking

Fieldgrid includes built‑in time tracking:

- **Time entries**
  - Represent when technicians clock in and out.
  - Can be linked to specific work orders or jobs.
  - Support statuses like draft, submitted, approved, or rejected.

This gives you an accurate picture of time spent in the field for payroll, job costing, and performance analysis.

## Checklists, logs, and evidence

To standardize and document work, Fieldgrid uses:

- **Checklists**
  - Step‑by‑step templates that define what needs to be done on a job or site.
  - Can require specific types of evidence before being marked complete.
- **Logs**
  - Records such as daily reports, incident notes, or maintenance summaries.
  - Capture what happened on site in a structured way.
- **Evidence**
  - Photos, notes, and signatures collected as proof of work.

These tools support compliance, quality, and clear communication between the office, technicians, and customers.

## Inventory and expenses

Fieldgrid helps you track materials and costs:

- **Inventory**
  - Items and stock levels such as parts, consumables, or equipment.
  - Includes part requests from technicians and records of what was actually used on each job.
- **Expenses**
  - Out‑of‑pocket costs like travel, tools, or materials purchased in the field.
  - Can be linked back to specific jobs or visits.

Both inventory and expenses flow into:

- Accurate job costing and profitability.
- Clear, itemized invoices for customers.

## Documents, resources, and library

Fieldgrid centralizes important documentation:

- **Resources**
  - Files attached to jobs, projects, or jobsites—such as drawings, photos, manuals, or site‑specific instructions.
- **Library**
  - A more general library of reference material: SOPs, safety protocols, training materials, and so on.

Technicians and office staff rely on these to:

- Find the right instructions at the right time.
- Maintain consistency across teams and locations.

## Notifications, activity, and announcements

Fieldgrid keeps everyone in sync with:

- **Notifications**
  - Personal alerts about assignments, comments, approvals, and schedule changes.
- **Activity and audit views**
  - A history of who changed what and when, especially useful for investigations and reviews.
- **Announcements**
  - Broadcast messages from admins or super admins to specific roles or entire organizations.

These features reduce surprises and help you understand what’s happening across your operation.

## AI assistant (Alex)

**Alex** is the AI layer on top of your Fieldgrid data:

- Lets you ask questions in plain language about jobs, documents, time, and more.
- Helps summarize long histories and documents.
- Suggests next steps or highlights patterns, while leaving final decisions in your hands.

Alex uses your existing jobs, logs, and resources as context, so its answers are grounded in your real data.

## How concepts fit together

At a high level:

- Organizations contain **customers**, which contain **projects**, which contain **jobs** and **jobsites**.
- Jobs and jobsites host **work orders**, **time entries**, **checklists**, **logs**, **resources**, and **expenses**.
- Users with different roles interact with these concepts through web and mobile experiences.
- Notifications, announcements, and Alex sit on top to orchestrate and enhance everyday workflows.
