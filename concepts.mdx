## Organizations, users, and roles

Fieldgrid is multi-tenant: everything happens inside an **organization**.

- **Organizations**
  - Represent a customer account that owns projects, jobs, users, and configuration.
  - Modeled in the backend under `app/organizations` and exposed via `/api/v1/organizations`.
  - Frontend type: `Organization` in `fieldgrid-react/src/types/index.ts`.
- **Users**
  - People who log into Fieldgrid.
  - Carry a platform role such as `owner`, `admin`, `technician`, `project_manager`, `supervisor`, or `superadmin` (`User.role` in `src/types/index.ts` and backend roles).
  - Belong to one or more organizations and may have organization-specific roles and permissions.
- **Roles and permissions**
  - Roles such as **admin**, **technician**, **project_manager**, and **supervisor** shape what users can see and do.
  - Route-level access is enforced in the frontend via `canAccessRoute` (see `src/utils/routing.ts`) and in the backend via role-aware endpoints.

## Customers, projects, jobs, and work orders

Fieldgrid models operational work in a hierarchy, as described in the backend README:

> Customer → Project → Job → Work Order, with Jobsites attached to jobs.

- **Customers**
  - Client accounts you serve.
  - Backend module: `app/customers` with `/api/v1/customers`.
  - Frontend type: `Customer` in `src/types/index.ts`.
- **Projects**
  - Group related jobs for a customer.
  - Carry dates, budgets, and a project manager.
  - Backend module: `app/projects` with `/api/v1/projects`.
  - Frontend type: `Project` in `src/types/index.ts`.
- **Jobs**
  - Operational units within a project.
  - Represent a body of work with status, priority, locations, and assignments.
  - Backend module: `app/jobs` with `/api/v1/jobs`.
  - Frontend type: `Job` in `src/types/index.ts`.
- **Work Orders**
  - Field-execution units (tasks/subtasks) assigned to technicians.
  - Backend module: `app/work_orders` with `/api/v1/work-orders`.
  - Frontend type: `WorkOrder` in `src/types/index.ts`.
- **Jobsites**
  - Physical locations linked to jobs.
  - Backend module: `app/jobsites` with `/api/v1/jobsites`.
  - Frontend types: `Jobsite` and `JobsiteDetails` in `src/types/index.ts`.

These concepts show up throughout the UI:

- Admins work with customers, projects, jobs, and work orders via `/admin` pages.
- Technicians see their assigned jobs and work orders under `/technician` routes.

## Time tracking

Fieldgrid includes built-in time tracking:

- **Time entries**
  - Represent clock-in/clock-out sessions for technicians, often linked to work orders.
  - Backend: `app/clock` with `/api/v1/clock`.
  - Frontend type: `TimeEntry` in `src/types/index.ts`.
  - Track:
    - Start and end times.
    - Breaks.
    - Statuses like `draft`, `submitted`, `approved`, `rejected`.
  - Used by:
    - Technicians (for daily workflow and clock screens).
    - Admins (for approvals and reporting).

## Checklists, logs, and evidence

To standardize and document work, Fieldgrid uses:

- **Checklists**
  - Templates that define steps and required evidence for a given job or work type.
  - Backend: `app/checklists` with `/api/v1/checklists`.
  - Frontend types:
    - `Checklist`, `ChecklistItem`, `ChecklistTemplate`, `ChecklistInstance`, `ChecklistInstanceItem` in `src/types/index.ts`.
- **Logs**
  - Site logs such as daily reports, incidents, maintenance notes, and inspections.
  - Backend: modeled as **Logs** in the database schema.
  - Frontend type: `Log` in `src/types/index.ts`.
- **Evidence**
  - Photos, notes, and signatures captured as proof of work.
  - Frontend type: `Evidence` in `src/types/index.ts`.

These concepts support compliance, quality assurance, and communication across jobs and jobsites.

## Inventory and expenses

Fieldgrid tracks materials and job-related costs via:

- **Inventory**
  - Items, stock levels, and part requests.
  - Backend: inventory modules and endpoints (for example, item and transaction APIs).
  - Frontend types:
    - Inventory and part requests in `src/types/inventory/*`.
- **Expenses**
  - Out-of-pocket costs like travel, tools, or materials.
  - Backend: expense endpoints.
  - Frontend types: `Expense`, `ExpenseAttachment`, and related filter/summary types in `src/types/index.ts`.

Inventory and expenses feed into:

- Job costing and profitability.
- Invoice line items for customers.

## Documents, resources, and library

Fieldgrid centralizes documents and reference material:

- **Resources**
  - Documents, media, and site files attached to jobs, projects, or jobsites.
  - Backend: `app/resources`.
  - Frontend types: `resources` domain in `src/types/resources/resources.ts`.
- **Library**
  - A curated library of documents such as manuals, procedures, and safety protocols.
  - Frontend types: `LibraryDocument`, `LibraryCategory`, and `LibraryFilter` in `src/types/index.ts`.

Resources and library entries are used by:

- Technicians in the field to access instructions.
- The AI assistant (Alex) as context for answering questions.

## Notifications, activities, and announcements

Fieldgrid keeps users informed with:

- **Notifications**
  - In-app alerts sent to specific users.
  - Backend: `app/notifications`.
  - Frontend types: `AppNotification` and domain-specific notification types.
- **Activities and audit logs**
  - Track who did what and when for jobs and other entities.
  - Backend: activity and audit modules.
  - Frontend types: `JobLogEntry` and activity types in `src/types/activities/*`.
- **Announcements**
  - Broadcast messages to organizations or globally (especially via super admin).
  - Frontend types: `announcements` domain in `src/types/announcements/announcements.ts`.

## AI assistant (Alex)

Alex is the AI layer on top of Fieldgrid’s data:

- Backend: `app/alex` and AI endpoints under `/api/v1/ai` and `/api/v1/alex`.
- Frontend types:
  - `AIMessage`, `AIConversation` in `src/types/index.ts`.
  - Additional types under `src/types/ai/*` and `src/types/alex/*`.

Alex uses:

- Job, jobsite, checklist, and document context to answer questions.
- Uploaded documents for retrieval-augmented generation (RAG).

## How concepts fit together

- Organizations contain **customers**, which contain **projects**, which contain **jobs** and **jobsites**, which in turn have **work orders**, **checklists**, **time entries**, **logs**, **resources**, and **expenses**.
- Users with various **roles** interact with these entities through the web app, mobile app, and APIs.
- Notifications, announcements, and AI sit on top to orchestrate and enhance workflows.
