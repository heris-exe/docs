## Who this is for

This page is for people who are responsible for **running day-to-day operations**:

- Service managers and coordinators.
- Office admins and dispatchers.
- Anyone who needs a clear picture of what’s happening in the field.

## Problems Fieldgrid helps you solve

- You can’t easily see **which jobs are open**, who’s assigned, and what’s blocked.
- Work details live in **spreadsheets, texts, and calls**, making handoffs fragile.
- It’s hard to connect **time, materials, and invoices** into one clean picture.
- You spend too much time **answering “what’s going on with…?”** questions.

## How Fieldgrid changes your day

### One place to see and manage work

- A single dashboard shows:
  - Active projects and jobs.
  - Upcoming work and priorities.
  - Recent activity and alerts.
- You can quickly:
  - Create and assign jobs and work orders.
  - See which visits are at risk of running late.
  - Drill into details when something needs attention.

See: **Admin job lifecycle** and **Admin dashboard and navigation** guides.

### Confident scheduling and assignments

- Use scheduling views to:
  - See who is available and where they’re working.
  - Balance workloads across technicians and teams.
  - Adjust plans as priorities change without losing the big picture.

Technicians see the same reality in their own views, so there’s less confusion and fewer calls.

See: **Admin scheduling and resources** and **Technician daily workflow**.

### Time, inventory, and expenses tied to real work

- Technicians:
  - Clock in and out.
  - Record time against jobs.
  - Request and use parts.
  - Log expenses like mileage or parking.
- You:
  - See how much time and material each job actually used.
  - Get cleaner job costing and fewer surprises later.

See: **Technician time tracking**, **Technician inventory and expenses**, and **Admin inventory management**.

### Faster, clearer invoicing

- Turn completed work into invoices with:
  - Labor pulled from time entries.
  - Materials pulled from inventory usage.
  - Any additional fees or discounts.
- Track invoice status so you know:
  - What’s draft, sent, paid, or overdue.

See: **Admin invoicing and billing**.

### Built-in communication and context

- Notifications keep people in the loop about:
  - New assignments.
  - Status changes.
  - Approvals and comments.
- Logs, checklists, and photos mean:
  - You have a clear record of what happened on site.
  - It’s easier to answer customer questions with confidence.

See: **Notifications and announcements** and **Admin job lifecycle**.

### Extra help from Alex, the AI assistant

- Ask Alex to:
  - Summarize what’s happened on a job or project.
  - Surface key points from logs and documents.
  - Help prepare updates for customers or leadership.

See: **AI assistant (Alex)**.

## Where to start as an admin

If you’re evaluating or just getting started:

1. Read **What is Fieldgrid?** and **Fieldgrid concepts**.
2. Follow the **Admin onboarding** and **Admin getting started** guides.
3. Try an end-to-end run:
   - Create a customer, project, and job.
   - Assign a technician and work orders.
   - Log time and materials.
   - Generate an invoice.

As you go, keep an eye on the dashboard—this is the view you’ll live in day to day.
