## Overview

Fieldgrid includes built-in **time tracking** so technicians can:

- Clock in and out for their day.
- Log time against jobs and work orders.
- Capture breaks and travel time where applicable.

Technician-facing time tracking lives primarily under `/technician/clock` and related pages, with admin review surfaces at `/admin/time-entries`.

## Entry points

- Technician:
  - `/technician/clock` → clock in/out and current status.
  - `/technician/schedule` and job views → context for where time is spent.
- Admin:
  - `/admin/time-entries` → time approvals and reporting.

Routing and access control are shared with the rest of the `/technician` and `/admin` namespaces, governed by `ProtectedRoute` and `canAccessRoute`.

## Technician flow

### 1. Clock in

On `/technician/clock`:

- The technician sees:
  - Current clock status (clocked in vs clocked out).
  - Any active job context (if applicable).
- When they click **Clock in**:
  - The UI calls `useClock` mutations to:
    - Create a new time entry.
    - Optionally associate it with a job or work order.

### 2. Work on assigned jobs

- As they move through jobs:
  - Time may be:
    - Logged continuously (one long shift).
    - Switched between jobs or work orders using job log views.
- Job-level logs (e.g., `/technician/jobs/:id/log`) can surface:
  - Time summaries.
  - Links to time entry details where needed.

### 3. Breaks and clock out

- From `/technician/clock`:
  - Technicians can:
    - Start or end breaks (if modeled in the backend).
    - Clock out at the end of their shift.
- The frontend updates:
  - `TimeEntry` records via clock APIs.
  - Local UI state in `useClock` and related hooks.

## Admin review flow

### 1. View time entries

On `/admin/time-entries`:

- Admins see:
  - Time entries grouped by technician, date, or job.
  - Statuses (pending, approved, rejected).
  - Summaries for payroll or billing.

### 2. Approve and correct

- Admin actions typically include:
  - Approving or rejecting entries.
  - Editing time when corrections are needed, subject to permissions.
- These operations are implemented via:
  - `clock` admin APIs (`team` endpoints, where present).
  - Admin-specific hooks or mutations around `useClock` or dedicated hooks.

### 3. Downstream impact

- Approved time entries can drive:
  - Payroll calculations.
  - Invoicing (labor line items).
  - Analytics and utilization metrics.
