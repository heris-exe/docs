## What technicians can do

Fieldgrid gives technicians a simple way to keep accurate records of their time:

- **Clock in and out** at the beginning and end of the day.
- **Log time against specific jobs or visits**, when needed.
- **Capture breaks and travel time** where your process requires it.

All of this happens through a dedicated clock experience and subtle prompts throughout the day.

## Starting the day: clock in

When a technician begins work, they:

1. Open Fieldgrid on their device.
2. Go to the clock area.
3. Tap **Clock in**.

They immediately see that they are “on the clock,” along with any context about what they’re currently working on (for example, their first job of the day).

## During the day: time against jobs

As technicians move through their assigned work:

- They can see which jobs or visits are planned for the day.
- When they start work on a job, Fieldgrid can:
  - Associate their active time with that job or visit.
  - Show how much time they’ve already spent there.

This makes it easy for the office to see where time is going, without technicians needing to fill out complex timesheets.

## Breaks and travel

If your organization tracks breaks and travel time, technicians can:

- Start and end a break from the same clock view.
- Capture travel between jobs, according to your policies.

This helps keep payroll, utilization, and job costing more accurate.

## Ending the day: clock out

At the end of the shift, technicians:

1. Open the clock view again.
2. Tap **Clock out**.

Fieldgrid closes out the active time entry, so the day’s work is clearly defined and ready for review.

## What admins and managers see

In the admin view, managers can:

- See time entries by technician, date, or job.
- Spot missing clocks (for example, clocked in but never clocked out).
- Review total hours for payroll or billing.

Where your process requires it, admins can approve or adjust entries, helping prevent mistakes before they affect invoices or pay runs.

## How this connects to the rest of Fieldgrid

Time tracking doesn’t live in isolation:

- It explains **how long work really takes** on specific jobs and work orders.
- It feeds into **invoicing** when labor appears as a line item.
- It supports **reporting and analytics** so you can spot trends and improve planning.
