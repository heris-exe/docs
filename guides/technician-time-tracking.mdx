## What technicians can do

Fieldgrid gives technicians a simple way to keep accurate records of their time:

- **Clock in and out** at the beginning and end of the day.
- **Log time against specific jobs or visits**, when needed.
- **Capture breaks and travel time** where your process requires it.

All of this happens through a dedicated clock experience and subtle prompts throughout the day.

## Starting the day: clock in

When a technician begins work, they:

1. Open Fieldgrid on their device.
2. Go to the clock area.
3. Tap **Clock in**.

They immediately see that they are “on the clock,” along with any context about what they’re currently working on (for example, their first job of the day).

## During the day: time against jobs

As technicians move through their assigned work:

- They can see which jobs or visits are planned for the day.
- When they start work on a job, Fieldgrid can:
  - Associate their active time with that job or visit.
  - Show how much time they’ve already spent there.

This makes it easy for the office to see where time is going, without technicians needing to fill out complex timesheets.

## Breaks and travel

If your organization tracks breaks and travel time, technicians can:

- Start and end a break from the same clock view.
- Capture travel between jobs, according to your policies.

This helps keep payroll, utilization, and job costing more accurate.

## Ending the day: clock out

At the end of the shift, technicians:

1. Open the clock view again.
2. Tap **Clock out**.

Fieldgrid closes out the active time entry, so the day’s work is clearly defined and ready for review.

## What admins and managers see

In the admin view, managers can:

- See time entries by technician, date, or job.
- Spot missing clocks (for example, clocked in but never clocked out).
- Review total hours for payroll or billing.

Where your process requires it, admins can approve or adjust entries, helping prevent mistakes before they affect invoices or pay runs.

### Approving and correcting time

On the admin side, time usually flows through a simple review cycle:

1. **Technicians submit or finalize** their time for the day.
2. Admins review:
   - Total hours per technician.
   - How time is split across jobs and work orders.
3. If something looks wrong:
   - Adjust the entry directly (for example, change the job or duration), or
   - Send a note to the technician to clarify what happened.
4. Once everything looks right, mark entries as **approved** so they’re ready for payroll and invoicing.

This review step is your main safeguard before time hits customer invoices or pay runs.

### Fixing common mistakes

Some patterns show up often and are easy to fix when caught early:

- **Forgot to clock out**
  - Admins can close the open entry at the correct time.
  - Add a short note so there’s a record of why it was adjusted.
- **Time logged to the wrong job**
  - Reassign the entry to the correct job or work order.
  - Confirm with the technician if there’s any doubt.
- **Breaks or travel not recorded**
  - Depending on your policy, you can:
    - Add a missing break.
    - Split an entry into “job time” and “travel” for better costing.

Encourage technicians to flag mistakes as soon as they notice them; it’s much easier to correct yesterday’s shift than last month’s.

## How this connects to the rest of Fieldgrid

Time tracking doesn’t live in isolation:

- It explains **how long work really takes** on specific jobs and work orders.
- It feeds into **invoicing** when labor appears as a line item.
- It supports **reporting and analytics** so you can spot trends and improve planning.

## Scenarios and playbooks

### Scenario: Standard T&M service day

For a technician working a typical time‑and‑materials day:

1. **Morning**
   - Clock in from the main clock view.
   - Open the first job from the dashboard or schedule.
2. **On each job**
   - Make sure the active time is associated with the current job or visit.
   - If you leave for another job, switch the active context so time follows you.
3. **Breaks and travel**
   - Start a break when you stop working.
   - Record travel between jobs if your company tracks it.
4. **End of day**
   - Clock out from the clock view.
   - Quickly review your day’s jobs to confirm they reflect what you actually did.

Admins will then approve your time and use it to generate T&M invoices where appropriate.

### Scenario: Fixed‑fee project day

Even when the customer is paying a flat fee, your time still matters internally.

1. Follow the same pattern as above:
   - Clock in, associate time with jobs, clock out.
2. For each fixed‑fee job:
   - Log time accurately so project managers can see how much effort the work really took.
3. Admins and leaders:
   - Use this time data to compare **actual effort** vs. **quoted effort**.
   - Adjust pricing or scopes for future similar projects.

From the technician’s point of view, nothing changes—you still use the clock the same way. The difference is in how admins and leaders use the resulting data.
