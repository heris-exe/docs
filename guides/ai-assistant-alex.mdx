## What Alex is

**Alex** is Fieldgrid’s built‑in AI assistant. It sits on top of your jobs, documents, and processes and helps your team:

- Get answers to questions about work in plain language.
- Summarize long trails of activity or documentation.
- Spot patterns or next steps without digging through multiple screens.

You can think of Alex as a teammate that “knows” your Fieldgrid workspace.

## Where you’ll see Alex

Alex appears in a few key places:

- **Dedicated AI page**
  - A focused space where admins or managers can ask questions, review past conversations, and explore insights.
- **In‑context entry points**
  - A button or icon on screens like jobs, projects, schedules, or resources that says something like **Ask Alex**.
  - When clicked, it opens Alex with that job or context already in mind.

## Common ways to use Alex

### 1. Ask questions about jobs and projects

From a job or project, you might ask:

- “What’s the current status of this job?”
- “Which visits are still open this week?”
- “Has anything blocked this project in the last month?”

Alex can use the information in Fieldgrid to respond in a way that’s easy to understand, without you needing to run separate reports.

### 2. Summarize activity and documents

When there’s a lot of history or documentation, Alex can help by:

- Summarizing long site logs or comment threads.
- Turning a set of notes into a short update you can share with a customer or manager.
- Highlighting key points from important documents (such as SOPs or manuals you’ve uploaded).

### 3. Get suggestions and next steps

Alex can also help with:

- Suggesting checks or follow‑up actions for common situations (for example, repeated callbacks on a job).
- Pointing out patterns in time tracking or job completion that might warrant attention.

These are suggestions, not automated changes—you stay in control.

## Who benefits from Alex

- **Technicians** can:
  - Quickly clarify steps in the field.
  - Get guidance without leaving the job they’re working on.
- **Admins and coordinators** can:
  - Shorten the time it takes to piece together what’s happening across multiple jobs.
  - Prepare updates and summaries faster.
- **Leaders** can:
  - Ask Alex high‑level questions about performance and trends, then drill into specific areas as needed.

## How to introduce Alex to your team

When rolling Alex out:

- Start with a few **clear examples** of questions and tasks it’s good at.
- Encourage people to:
  - Ask specific questions (for example, “Summarize this week’s work on Project X”).
  - Use Alex when they’d normally spend time hunting across multiple screens.
- Remind everyone that AI answers should still be **sanity‑checked** before making big decisions.

Over time, Alex becomes a natural part of how your team interacts with Fieldgrid—another way to get value out of the data you’re already collecting.
