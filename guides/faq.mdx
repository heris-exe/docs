## Getting access and login

### How do I get an account?

Your Fieldgrid admin or service manager invites you by email. If your company allows it, you may also see a “Create account” option on the login page. If you’re not sure, ask your manager which path your organization uses.

### I didn’t receive my invitation or verification email. What should I do?

- Check your spam or promotions folders.
- Search your inbox for “Fieldgrid”.
- If you still don’t see it, ask your admin to resend the invite or verification email.

See: **Signing in, MFA, and email verification** and **Technician onboarding** / **Admin onboarding**.

### I forgot my password. How do I reset it?

On the login page, click **Forgot password**, enter your email, and follow the link in the reset email. If the email doesn’t arrive after a few minutes, double‑check the address you entered and then contact your admin or support.

## Onboarding and invitations

### I’m an admin. What should I do first?

- Set up your organization using the admin onboarding flow.
- Invite a small set of admins and technicians.
- Run a test job end-to-end.

See: **Admin getting started** and **Admin onboarding: create a new organization**.

### I’m a technician. What should I do first?

- Accept your invite or join your company.
- Sign in and explore your dashboard and schedule.
- Walk through one complete job using the steps in the technician getting started guide.

See: **Technician onboarding** and **Technician getting started**.

## Jobs, work orders, and scheduling

### What’s the difference between a project, a job, and a work order?

- **Project** – a larger body of work for a customer.
- **Job** – a concrete unit of work under a project (for example, an installation or major visit).
- **Work order** – the specific tasks or visits technicians complete in the field.

See: **Fieldgrid concepts** and **Admin job lifecycle**.

### How do I know what I’m supposed to work on today?

- Technicians:
  - Use your **dashboard** and **schedule** to see today’s jobs and assignments.
- Admins:
  - Use the **Jobs** and **Schedule** views to see what’s assigned across the team.

See: **Technician daily workflow** and **Admin dashboard and navigation**.

## Time tracking and approvals

### How do technicians track their time?

Technicians:

- Clock in at the start of their day.
- Clock out at the end.
- May also record breaks and travel, depending on your process.

See: **Technician time tracking**.

### How do admins review and approve time?

Admins use a time or approvals view to:

- See entries by technician, job, or date.
- Approve or correct entries as needed.

Approved entries feed into payroll, job costing, and sometimes invoicing.

## Inventory, expenses, and billing

### How are parts and materials tracked?

- Inventory captures:
  - What items you stock.
  - Where they’re stored.
  - What gets used on which jobs.
- Technicians can request parts and record what they used.

See: **Admin inventory management** and **Technician inventory and expenses**.

### How do expenses work?

Technicians can log expenses (for example, fuel, parking, or materials) and link them to jobs. Admins can then review and approve them, and optionally push them into billing.

### How do we create invoices?

Admins use the invoicing area to:

- Pull in labor and materials from jobs.
- Add any additional line items.
- Generate and track invoices as draft, sent, paid, or overdue.

See: **Admin invoicing and billing**.

## AI assistant (Alex)

### What can Alex help with?

Alex can:

- Answer questions about jobs, activity, and documents.
- Summarize long histories or site logs.
- Suggest next steps or highlight patterns, while leaving final decisions to you.

See: **AI assistant (Alex)**.

### Is Alex making changes to our data?

Alex is designed to help you understand and act on your data, not to silently change it. Any actions that modify data should still go through your normal Fieldgrid workflows.
