## Who this is for

This page is for people who **do the work in the field**:

- Technicians.
- Field engineers.
- Anyone who needs clear instructions and an easy way to record what they did.

## Problems Fieldgrid helps you solve

- You’re juggling **texts, calls, and paper notes** to know what’s next.
- Job details are **unclear or scattered**, leading to rework and frustration.
- You’re expected to **fill out forms and timesheets** that don’t fit how the day actually goes.
- It’s hard to show **proof of work** when questions come up later.

## How Fieldgrid changes your day

### Start the day with a clear dashboard

- See **today’s jobs** and what’s coming next.
- Spot:
  - Where you need to be.
  - What kind of work each visit involves.
  - Any special instructions or notes.

See: **Technician daily workflow**.

### Know exactly what to do on each job

- For each job or work order, you can:
  - Read the scope of work and any checklists.
  - See jobsite details and directions.
  - Review attached documents like drawings, SOPs, or photos.

No more guessing or hunting through separate apps for the information you need.

See: **Admin job lifecycle** and **Technician daily workflow**.

### Record work as you go

- As you complete work:
  - Update work order statuses (for example, in progress → done).
  - Fill out checklists step by step.
  - Add notes about what you found or fixed.
  - Attach photos for before/after proof.

This creates a trustworthy record for your team and your customers—without separate paperwork.

### Time tracking that fits the way you work

- Clock in and out from a simple view.
- Link time to jobs when needed.
- Capture breaks and travel time where your company requires it.

You don’t have to remember everything at the end of the day; Fieldgrid helps keep time aligned with actual work.

See: **Technician time tracking**.

### Parts and expenses without the chaos

- Request parts when you need them, so the office can see:
  - What you asked for.
  - Why you need it.
- Record:
  - Which parts you actually used.
  - Expenses like fuel, parking, or materials you purchased.

This helps the office keep inventory accurate and makes sure you’re not chasing reimbursements later.

See: **Technician inventory and expenses**.

### Less back-and-forth, more clarity

- Notifications and logs mean:
  - You’re alerted when something important changes.
  - There’s a clear history of comments and updates for each job.
- If questions come up later:
  - The team can look back at what was recorded instead of interrupting you in the field.

See: **Notifications and announcements**.

## Where to start as a technician

If you’re new to Fieldgrid:

1. Make sure your account and access are set up (see **Technician onboarding**).
2. Explore your **dashboard** and **schedule** to see today’s work.
3. Walk through one complete cycle:
   - Open a job.
   - Follow the instructions and checklists.
   - Log notes, photos, time, and any parts/expenses.
   - Mark the work as done.

The goal is to make Fieldgrid feel like a natural part of your day—not another system you have to fight with.
