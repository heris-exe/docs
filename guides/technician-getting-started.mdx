## Who this is for

This guide is for **technicians and field staff** who are new to Fieldgrid and want to know:

- How to sign in.
- Where to see today’s work.
- How to record what they did without extra paperwork.

## First login and setup

1. **Accept your invite (or join your company)**
   - Follow: **Technician onboarding: join an existing organization**.
   - Make sure you:
     - Accept your email invite, or
     - Complete the “join company” steps if your company uses self‑service join.

2. **Sign in and secure your account**
   - Follow: **Signing in, MFA, and email verification**.
   - Confirm you can:
     - Log in from your main device.
     - Access your dashboard.

## Your first day in Fieldgrid

3. **Get to know your dashboard**
   - Read: **Technician daily workflow** (Dashboard section).
   - On your dashboard, look for:
     - Today’s jobs.
     - Any important messages or alerts.

4. **Check your schedule**
   - In the schedule view, confirm:
     - Which jobs you have today.
     - Where you need to be and when.

5. **Open one job and follow it through**
   - From your jobs list:
     - Open a job assigned to you.
     - Review:
       - Instructions and checklists.
       - Jobsite details and any documents.
   - As you work:
     - Update the work order status.
     - Fill out checklists.
     - Add notes or photos as needed.

## Recording time and expenses

6. **Clock in and out**
   - Read: **Technician time tracking**.
   - On your first day:
     - Clock in when you start.
     - Clock out when you’re done.
   - If your company tracks breaks or travel:
     - Practice starting/ending a break.

7. **Log any expenses**
   - Read: **Technician inventory and expenses**.
   - If you spend money on behalf of the company (for example, fuel or parking):
     - Create a test expense (if allowed) so you know how it works.

## Getting comfortable in your first week

8. **Use checklists and resources**
   - On jobs that have checklists:
     - Walk through each step carefully.
   - When you see a Resources or Documents area:
     - Open any drawings, SOPs, or reference files for the job.

9. **Watch how your updates help the team**
   - Ask your manager or coordinator to show you how:
     - Your status updates change what they see.
     - Your notes and photos help answer customer questions.

10. **Try asking Alex, the AI assistant (if enabled)**
    - Read: **AI assistant (Alex)**.
    - From a job or project, try asking a simple question like:
      - “Summarize what’s been done here so far.”

## Quick checklist for technicians

By the end of your first few days, you should be comfortable with:

- [ ] Signing in and out of Fieldgrid.
- [ ] Seeing today’s work on your dashboard and schedule.
- [ ] Opening a job and understanding what’s expected.
- [ ] Updating work orders and completing checklists.
- [ ] Clocking in/out and, if applicable, recording breaks.
- [ ] Logging expenses when needed.
- [ ] Using notes, photos, and documents to capture what you did.

Once these basics feel natural, you can explore more advanced features at your own pace, using the other guides as references.
