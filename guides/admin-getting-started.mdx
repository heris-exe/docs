## Who this is for

This guide is for **new admins and service managers** who are responsible for:

- Setting up Fieldgrid for their organization.
- Inviting the right people.
- Getting real jobs and invoices flowing through the system.

It gives you a suggested Day 1 / Week 1 path using the existing docs.

## Day 1 – Get access and set up your organization

1. **Sign in and secure your account**
   - Follow: **Signing in, MFA, and email verification**.
   - Make sure you can log in reliably and that email verification works.

2. **Create your organization**
   - Follow: **Admin onboarding: create a new organization**.
   - Enter your company details and choose how you want to start (trial or plan).

3. **Invite your core team**
   - From your workspace setup and dashboard:
     - Invite at least:
       - One other admin or coordinator.
       - One or two technicians for testing.

## Day 2 – Learn the basics of work management

4. **Understand the core concepts**
   - Read: **Fieldgrid concepts**.
   - Make sure terms like **customer**, **project**, **job**, **work order**, and **jobsite** are clear.

5. **Explore the admin dashboard**
   - Read: **Admin dashboard and navigation**.
   - Click through:
     - Dashboard.
     - Jobs.
     - Schedule.
     - Invoices and inventory.

6. **Walk through an example job lifecycle**
   - Read: **Admin job lifecycle and work orders**.
   - In your own workspace:
     - Create a test customer and project.
     - Create a job and some work orders.
     - Assign them to a test technician.

## Days 3–4 – Connect time, inventory, and billing

7. **See how technicians experience the day**
   - Pair with a technician or log in as a test account and read:
     - **Technician onboarding: join an existing organization**.
     - **Technician daily workflow**.

8. **Set up time tracking**
   - Read: **Technician time tracking**.
   - Decide:
     - How you want technicians to clock in/out.
     - Whether to track breaks and travel.

9. **Start modeling inventory and expenses**
   - Read:
     - **Admin inventory management**.
     - **Technician inventory and expenses**.
   - Add a small set of key parts and walk through:
     - A part request.
     - Recording usage on a job.

10. **Practice creating an invoice**
    - Read: **Admin invoicing and billing**.
    - From your test job:
      - Pull in time and materials where possible.
      - Generate a draft invoice to see the end-to-end flow.

## Day 5 – Add intelligence and communication

11. **Turn on notifications and announcements for your team**
    - Read: **Notifications and announcements**.
    - Decide:
      - Which notifications matter most.
      - How you want to use announcements (for example, for rollout updates).

12. **Experiment with Alex, your AI assistant**
    - Read: **AI assistant (Alex)**.
    - Try:
      - Asking Alex about a real or test job.
      - Having it summarize logs or activity you’ve just created.

## Week 1 checklist

By the end of your first week, aim to have:

- [ ] At least one real customer and project in Fieldgrid.
- [ ] Real jobs and work orders created and assigned.
- [ ] A small group of technicians using the app for at least part of their day.
- [ ] Time tracking configured and tested.
- [ ] A first invoice created (even if it’s a test).
- [ ] Notifications and announcements set up for your rollout.
- [ ] Alex tried at least once on a real job or project.

From here, you can deepen usage area by area, using the other guides as needed.
