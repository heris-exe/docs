---

## Who this is for

This guide is for **technicians and admins** who believe a job should be visible in Fieldgrid but can’t find it in their lists or schedule.

## Quick checks for technicians

1. **Check the date range**
   - Make sure your list or schedule is showing the correct day or week.
   - Try expanding the date range to see if the job is earlier or later than you expected.

2. **Check filters**
   - Look for any filters such as:
     - Status (for example, only showing “In progress”).
     - Customer, project, or location.
   - Clear filters and see if the job appears.

3. **Check your assignments**
   - Confirm with your manager that:
     - You are actually assigned to this job or work order.
     - The assignment hasn’t been moved to another technician.

If the job still doesn’t appear, share the job name and customer with your admin or coordinator so they can investigate.

## Quick checks for admins

1. **Verify the job exists and is open**
   - Search for the job by name or reference.
   - Confirm it hasn’t been completed, canceled, or archived.

2. **Check filters and views**
   - In job lists or boards:
     - Clear or adjust filters for:
       - Status.
       - Customer or project.
       - Assigned technician.
   - In schedule views:
     - Confirm the correct dates, teams, or locations are selected.

3. **Check technician assignments**
   - Open the job:
     - Confirm which technicians are assigned.
     - Verify that the technician who reported the issue is actually listed.

4. **Confirm organization and environment**
   - Make sure both you and the technician:
     - Are looking at the same organization.
     - Are using the same environment (for example, production vs. a test environment, if applicable).

## When to contact support

If you’ve gone through these checks and:

- The job exists, but still doesn’t show up for the right person.
- Filters and date ranges look correct.
- You’ve confirmed assignments and organization.

Then contact Fieldgrid support with:

- Organization name.
- Job name and any reference ID.
- Which user can’t see it, and what they expected to see.

Point them to this guide and the **Admin job lifecycle** and **Technician daily workflow** docs you’ve already reviewed.
