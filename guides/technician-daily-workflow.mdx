## Overview

This guide follows a technician through a typical day using Fieldgrid:

- Starting from the technician dashboard.
- Reviewing today’s jobs and schedule.
- Completing work orders and logging work.
- Using resources, photos, and logs to document the job.

## Entry points

After signing in, technicians land on their dashboard and can move into jobs, schedule, resources, inventory, invoices, expenses, announcements, and time tracking from there.

## Morning: review dashboard and schedule

### Dashboard

The technician dashboard gives a quick snapshot of the day, including:

- Today’s and upcoming jobs.
- Quick access to recently viewed work.
- Time tracking prompts or important alerts, depending on your organization’s setup.

### Schedule

The schedule view shows:

- Assigned jobs and work orders for the day or week.
- Time slots and any overlapping commitments.

## On the job: jobs and work orders

### 1. Open assigned jobs

In the jobs list, technicians can see:

- Their assigned jobs.
- Which customer or project the work belongs to.
- Current status and due dates.

### 2. View job details

When a technician opens a job, they can usually:

- See the related work orders.
- Review instructions and checklists.
- View jobsite information and directions.
- Open linked documents and resources.

### 3. Complete work orders and checklists

As work progresses, technicians can:

- Open a work order.
- Mark it as started, in progress, or complete.
- Fill out required checklists and forms.

This helps everyone stay aligned on what has been done and what still needs attention.

### 4. Log notes, photos, and site logs

While on site, technicians can add:

- Notes about the work completed.
- Photos.
- Documents or other supporting information.

This creates a useful record for office staff, future technicians, and the customer.

## Throughout the day: time and expenses

- Time tracking:
  - See **Technician time tracking** guide for details.
- Expenses:
  - Technicians can log job-related expenses such as mileage, parking, or materials when required.

Tips:

- Record information **as you go** rather than at the end of the day—this keeps details fresh and avoids missing items.
- Use photos generously when they help explain what you found, fixed, or installed.

## Wrap-up: review and next steps

At the end of the day, technicians:

- Review remaining jobs and upcoming work.
- Ensure time entries and key checklists are complete.
- Check resources or instructions if there are any final documentation requirements.

## Example: A full day for one technician

A typical day might look like:

1. Start on the dashboard:
   - See three jobs scheduled: a morning service, a midday install assist, and an afternoon inspection.
2. Head to the first job:
   - Open the job, review instructions and checklists.
   - Complete the work, capture notes and photos, and mark the work order done.
3. Move to the next jobs:
   - Repeat the pattern: open job → follow instructions → record what was done.
4. Throughout the day:
   - Use time tracking and expenses (when applicable) so the office doesn’t need to chase for details.
5. At the end of the day:
   - Review the dashboard and schedule to confirm everything is up to date.
   - Make sure no important notes or checklists were missed.

From the technician’s perspective, Fieldgrid becomes the single place to see what’s next and record what happened.
