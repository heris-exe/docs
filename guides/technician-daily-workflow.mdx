## Overview

This guide follows a technician through a typical day using Fieldgrid:

- Starting from the technician dashboard.
- Reviewing today’s jobs and schedule.
- Completing work orders and logging work.
- Using resources, photos, and logs to document the job.

The technician experience lives under the `/technician` route namespace in `fieldgrid-react`.

## Entry points

- After onboarding and login, technicians land on:
  - `/technician/dashboard`
- From there they can navigate to:
  - `/technician/jobs`
  - `/technician/projects`
  - `/technician/schedule`
  - `/technician/resources`
  - `/technician/inventory`
  - `/technician/invoices`
  - `/technician/expense`
  - `/technician/clock`
  - `/technician/settings`, `/technician/help`, `/technician/announcement`

Routing and access are handled via:

- `ProtectedRoute` and `canAccessRoute` in `src/App.tsx` and `src/utils/routing.ts`.

## Morning: review dashboard and schedule

### Dashboard

On `/technician/dashboard`:

- The technician sees:
  - Today’s and upcoming jobs.
  - Quick access to recently viewed work.
  - Time tracking or key alerts (depending on configuration).
- The page uses hooks like:
  - `useJobs` (filtered to technician assignments).
  - `useActivities`, `useNotifications`, or analytics hooks as applicable.

### Schedule

On `/technician/schedule`:

- The schedule view shows:
  - Assigned jobs and work orders for the period.
  - Time slots and overlapping assignments.

## On the job: jobs and work orders

### 1. Open assigned jobs

On `/technician/jobs`:

- The technician sees a list of:
  - Assigned jobs (via `useJobs` and backend filters).
  - Key fields: customer, project, status, due date.

### 2. View job details

For a specific job:

- `/technician/jobs/:id` → `TechnicianJobDetails`.
- Alternatively, `/jobs/:id` via `JobRouter`, which:
  - Picks the correct role-specific view based on user role.
- In job details, technicians can:
  - See work orders, checklists, and instructions.
  - View jobsite information and maps.
  - Access linked documents and resources.

### 3. Complete work orders and checklists

- In the **Work orders** area:
  - Technicians select a work order.
  - Update status (in progress, completed, etc.).
  - Fill out any checklists or forms.
- Checklists are backed by:
  - `checklists` types and APIs.
  - Hooks such as `useChecklists`, `useDailySafetyChecklist`.

### 4. Log notes, photos, and site logs

- Through job logs and resources:
  - Add site notes.
  - Attach photos or documents.
- These interactions rely on:
  - `resources` APIs and hooks (`useDocuments`, `useDocumentsQuery`).
  - Log or activity APIs (`useActivities`, `api/activities/*`).

## Throughout the day: time and expenses

- Time tracking:
  - See **Technician time tracking** guide for details.
- Expenses:
  - On `/technician/expense`:
    - Log expenses incurred on the job (mileage, materials, etc.).
  - Backed by:
    - `expenses` types and APIs (`src/types/expenses/*`, `src/api/expenses/expenses.ts`).

## Wrap-up: review and next steps

At the end of the day, technicians:

- Review remaining jobs on `/technician/jobs` and `/technician/schedule`.
- Ensure time entries and key checklists are complete.
- Consult `/technician/resources` for any post-work documentation requirements.
