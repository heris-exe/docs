## What these features do

Fieldgrid keeps teams informed in three main ways:

- **Notifications** – personal alerts about things that matter to you.
- **Announcements** – broadcasts from admins or super admins to a wider audience.
- **Activity and audit history** – a record of “who did what, when.”

Together, they reduce surprises, cut down on back‑and‑forth messages, and make it easier to understand what’s going on.

## Notifications

### 1. Receiving notifications

Users see notifications:

- In a notification icon or area in the header.
- Sometimes inline on pages (for example, when you’ve been assigned something new).

Common examples include:

- Being assigned to a new job or work order.
- A comment or note on work you’re responsible for.
- Approval or rejection of requests (time, expenses, parts, etc.).

### 2. Opening and marking as read

When someone clicks a notification, Fieldgrid typically:

- Takes them directly to the relevant job, project, invoice, or page.
- Marks that notification as read so it no longer appears as “new.”

This helps people jump straight to what needs their attention.

## Announcements

### 1. How admins and super admins use announcements

Admins and super admins can use announcements to:

- Share important updates with their teams (for example, policy changes or big releases).
- Highlight temporary changes (for example, scheduled maintenance or special instructions).
- Target messages to specific audiences, such as technicians only or the whole organization.

Announcements may appear:

- In a dedicated announcements area.
- As banners or cards on key pages.

### 2. How users see announcements

For everyday users, announcements show up as:

- A short list of current announcements relevant to them.
- Optional access to older announcements to understand what has changed recently.

## Activity and audit history

### 1. Why it matters

Activity and audit views answer questions like:

- “Who changed this setting?”
- “When was this status updated?”
- “What’s happened on this job in the last week?”

This is especially valuable when:

- Investigating issues or misunderstandings.
- Responding to customer questions about what was done and when.
- Supporting compliance or internal review processes.

### 2. What admins and super admins can see

Depending on their role, admins and super admins can:

- Filter history by user, job, project, or time frame.
- Drill into specific actions (for example, a change from one status to another).

This gives leaders and coordinators a clear, factual view of activity without needing to guess or reconstruct events from memory.

## How this fits into your workflows

- **Technicians** get timely, targeted notifications instead of relying on calls or texts.
- **Admins** can communicate clearly at scale and track how work changes over time.
- **Leaders** gain confidence that there’s a reliable history to refer back to when questions arise.

Used together, notifications, announcements, and activity history help Fieldgrid act as a single, shared source of truth for your operations.
