## Overview

Technicians often need to:

- Request parts and materials.
- Record which items they used on a job.
- Log out-of-pocket expenses such as fuel or parking.

This guide explains how those workflows work in the Fieldgrid technician UI, and how they tie back to admin inventory and billing.

## Entry points

- Inventory:
  - `/technician/inventory`
  - Contextual links from job or work order details.
- Expenses:
  - `/technician/expense`

- `TechnicianInventory` (`fieldgrid-react/src/pages/TechnicianInventory.tsx`)
- `TechnicianExpense` (`src/pages/TechnicianExpense.tsx`)

  - `TechnicianJobDetails.tsx`
  - `TechnicianJobLog.tsx`

## Inventory workflows

### 1. View available inventory

On `/technician/inventory`:

- Technicians can see:
  - Available items relevant to their jobs.
  - Stock information (depending on permissions).
- The list is populated via `useInventory` and `inventory` APIs.

### 2. Request parts

When a needed item is not readily available:

- From the technician inventory page or job context:
  - Submit a **part request**.
- The request includes:
  - Item details (name/code or selection from catalog).
  - Quantity requested.
  - Job or work order context.
- Requests are persisted via `part-requests` APIs and become visible to admins under admin inventory views.

### 3. Use parts on a job

During job execution:

- Technicians can:
  - Record that specific items were used.
  - Link those usages to:
    - A work order.
    - A job or project.
- These usages are reflected in:
  - Inventory transaction history.
  - Job costing and invoicing.

## Expense workflows

### 1. Log an expense

On `/technician/expense`:

- Technicians can log:
  - Reason (e.g., fuel, parking, supplies).
  - Amount and currency (if applicable).
  - Associated job or work order.
  - Photos of receipts (handled via resources or upload fields).
- Forms on this page:
  - Use `react-hook-form` and Zod schemas.
  - Call expense APIs through `expenses` modules.

### 2. Review and corrections

- Technicians can see:
  - Recent expenses they have submitted.
- Admins:
  - Review and approve expenses through admin-centric views (e.g., `/admin/expenses`).
  - Link approved expenses to invoices where appropriate.
