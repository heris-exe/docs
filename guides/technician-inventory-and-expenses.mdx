## What technicians can do

While doing work in the field, technicians often need to:

- Request specific parts or materials.
- Record which items they actually used.
- Log out-of-pocket expenses like fuel, parking, or supplies.

Fieldgrid brings all of this into one place so nothing gets lost in texts or paper receipts.

## Working with inventory

### 1. See available items

From the inventory area, technicians can:

- Browse items that are relevant to their work.
- See key information such as name, category, and (where allowed) stock information.

This helps them understand what’s available before they request or use anything.

### 2. Request parts

When a technician needs something that isn’t already on hand:

1. They open the inventory or job view.
2. Start a **part request**.
3. Fill in:
   - Which item they need (by name or selection).
   - How many.
   - Which job or visit it’s for.

Those requests appear for admins to triage and approve, so there’s a clear, trackable record of what was asked for and why.

### 3. Record parts used on a job

As work is completed:

- Technicians can indicate which parts they actually used.
- These usages are tied back to the relevant job or visit.

This makes it much easier to:

- Understand job costs.
- Confirm that inventory counts and billing line items stay in sync.

## Logging expenses

### 1. Capture expenses as they happen

From the expenses area, technicians can record costs such as:

- Fuel and mileage.
- Parking fees.
- Small tool or supply purchases.

Each expense typically includes:

- A description or category.
- Amount and currency.
- Which job or visit it relates to.
- A photo of the receipt, when required.

### 2. Keep a personal record

Technicians can usually see a short history of their recent expenses so they know:

- What they’ve submitted.
- What may still be pending review.

If something looks wrong after you submit it (for example, a typo in the amount), let an admin know so they can correct or reject it before it flows into billing or payroll.

## What admins and managers see

On the admin side:

- Part requests arrive in an approval queue.
- Approved requests can be turned into inventory movements.
- Expenses can be reviewed, approved, and tied into billing where necessary.

This gives finance and operations teams a clean, auditable picture of what was used and what it cost—without adding friction to the technician’s day.

## How this connects to the rest of Fieldgrid

- Inventory usage and expenses enrich the **job lifecycle**, showing the full cost of work.
- They feed into **invoicing and billing**, so customer invoices reflect both labor and materials.
- They give managers better insight into **profitability** and **field spending patterns**.

## Scenarios and playbooks

### Scenario: Large parts‑heavy job

On a big installation or repair with many parts:

1. Before leaving the shop:
   - Review the job and any attached materials list.
   - Request key parts in advance if they’re not already staged for you.
2. On site:
   - As you use parts, update usage in the job or inventory view rather than trying to remember everything at the end.
   - Take photos where helpful (for example, old vs. new components).
3. If you discover you’re missing something:
   - Create a new part request instead of just texting the office.
4. At the end of the job:
   - Double‑check that your recorded usage matches what actually left the truck or site.

This gives admins a complete picture of materials used and makes it easier to bill accurately.

### Scenario: Tech bought materials on the way

Sometimes you have to stop at a supplier or hardware store.

1. After paying:
   - Open the **expenses** area.
   - Create a new expense tied to the job or visit.
   - Enter:
     - A clear description (for example, “Conduit fittings – local supply house”).
     - The total amount.
     - A photo of the receipt.
2. If the material is something your company will stock going forward:
   - Mention this in the description or in a note so admins know to add it to inventory.
3. If you mis‑typed something:
   - Let your admin know quickly so they can fix or reject the entry and keep records clean.

This keeps you from losing receipts and ensures you’re reimbursed while still giving admins what they need for inventory and billing.

### Scenario: Simple service call with a few parts

For a typical quick service visit:

1. Bring your usual stock of common parts.
2. On the job:
   - Record only the parts you actually used—not everything on the truck.
3. If you use small consumables (for example, tape, screws) that your company bills as a generic fee:
   - Follow your company’s policy:
     - Either record them individually, or
     - Rely on a standard “materials fee” line in invoicing.

Fieldgrid doesn’t force one approach, but it gives you the tools to align with whatever your office decides.
