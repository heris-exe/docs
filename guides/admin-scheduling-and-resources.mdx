## Overview

Admins use Fieldgrid to coordinate **when work happens** and **which resources technicians use**.

This guide covers:

- Scheduling jobs and work orders.
- Viewing and managing schedules.
- Managing shared resources and documents.

The main surfaces are `/admin/schedule` and `/admin/resources` in the web app.

## Entry points

- **Schedule**
  - `/admin/schedule` → admin scheduling view.
- **Resources**
  - `/admin/resources` → document and resources library.
- From jobs, projects, and work orders:
  - Contextual links to schedule views.
  - Attachments and document references.

## Key screens

### Schedule

- Page: `AdminSchedule` (`fieldgrid-react/src/pages/AdminSchedule.tsx`).
- Typically shows:
  - Job and work-order assignments plotted over time.
  - Technician availability.
  - Filters by project, customer, or technician.

### Resources

- Page: `AdminResources` (`fieldgrid-react/src/pages/AdminResources.tsx`).
- Components:
  - `ResourcesFolderTreeView.tsx` – hierarchical folder navigation.
  - `DocumentFileIcon.tsx`, `DocumentAddModal.tsx`, `DocumentBatchUploadModal.tsx` – document workflows.

- Types:

## Scheduling workflows

### 1. View the schedule

In `/admin/schedule`, admins can:

- See a calendar or timeline of:
  - Jobs.
  - Work orders.
  - Assignments by technician.
- Use filters and time-range selectors to focus on:
  - Specific projects or customers.
  - Individual technicians or teams.

### 2. Assign and adjust work

- From the schedule view:
  - Create new assignments for jobs or work orders.
  - Drag-drop or edit existing entries (if supported by the UI).
- Under the hood:
  - Changes are persisted through `jobs/schedules` APIs.
  - Updates are reflected in technician dashboards and time tracking flows.

### 3. Coordinate with other modules

- Scheduling integrates with:
  - **Job lifecycle** – ensuring that scheduled work lines up with job statuses.
  - **Time tracking** – linking scheduled work to actual time entries.
  - **Notifications** – informing technicians of schedule changes.

## Resource management workflows

### 1. Organize resources

- Admins can create and manage a folder structure:
  - Per customer or project.
  - Per job type or standard operating procedure (SOP) category.
- `ResourcesFolderTreeView` and related components expose:
  - Folder hierarchy.
  - Document lists.

### 2. Add and update documents

- Using `DocumentAddModal` or `DocumentBatchUploadModal`, admins can:
  - Upload new files (images, PDFs, etc.).
  - Add metadata or tags.
- Thumbnails and previews are supported via:
  - `useDocumentThumbnail.ts` and UI components such as `DocumentFileIcon.tsx`.

### 3. Link resources to jobs and work orders

- Documents can be referenced in:
  - Job details.
  - Work order instructions.
  - Technician resources screens.

This creates a single source of truth for:

- Checklists and SOPs.
- Manuals and drawings.
- Photos and historical records.
