## Overview

The **admin dashboard** is the primary control center for operations leaders. From here admins can:

- See an overview of active work.
- Jump into jobs, projects, resources, analytics, and billing.
- Navigate across all admin surfaces using the left sidebar and top header.

## Entry points

After an admin signs in and completes any required onboarding, they land on the admin dashboard. From there, they can use the sidebar and header to move between different parts of the product.

## Layout shell

The admin experience shares a common layout across all pages:

- **Sidebar** with sectioned navigation (Dashboard, Jobs, Projects, Resources, Inventory, Invoices, Time, Customers, Analytics, Audit Log, Settings, Integrations, Help).
- **Header** with:
  - Organization selector.
  - Global search.
  - Notifications and announcements.
  - User menu and Alex (AI assistant) entry points.
- **Main content area** where the current page is displayed.

## Core admin routes

From the sidebar, admins can quickly reach:

- **Dashboard** – a high‑level overview of active jobs, activity, and any key alerts.
- **Jobs** – lists and filters for all jobs in progress.
- **Projects** – project portfolios grouped by customer.
- **Resources** – shared documents, drawings, and SOPs.
- **Inventory** – parts and materials.
- **Invoices** – operational invoicing for customers.
- **Expenses** – expense approvals.
- **Time** – time entries and approvals.
- **Schedule** – calendars or timelines for jobs and technicians.
- **Customers** – customer records and contact information.
- **Team** – user and role management.
- **Analytics** – reporting and insights.
- **Audit Log** – historical record of changes.
- **Settings** – organization‑level configuration.
- **Integrations** – connections to external tools.
- **Help** – guidance and support links.

## Example: A morning check-in ritual

Many service managers start their day like this:

1. Open the **Dashboard** to see:
   - Today’s jobs and any overdue work.
   - Recent important activity.
2. Jump into **Jobs** to:
   - Filter by “today” or “this week”.
   - Check for unassigned or at‑risk work.
3. Open **Schedule** to:
   - Confirm technicians are assigned to the right visits.
   - Move work around if there are conflicts.

You can then keep this view open while the day unfolds, using it as your live picture of operations.

## Navigation patterns

- **Sidebar navigation**
  - Use the left sidebar to move between major areas of the app.
  - Items may appear or disappear depending on your role and plan.
- **Breadcrumbs**
  - Shown near the top of each page to help you understand where you are.
- **Command palette / search**
  - Lets you quickly jump to jobs, projects, customers, or other entities by name.
  - Often triggered via a keyboard shortcut or a search box in the header.
